# Situation: Starting a New Leadership Role

**When this applies:** You've just taken on a new leadership position — promotion, lateral move, or new organization — and need to establish yourself effectively in the first 90 days.

---

## What the Wiki Recommends

The first 90 days are the highest-leverage and highest-risk period of any leadership role. Success requires learning before acting, building relationships before pushing change, and securing [[early-wins]] that create credibility for larger moves.

---

## Step-by-Step Approach

### Week 1 — Diagnose the Situation

1. **Identify your STARS situation** (Watkins): Is this a Start-up, Turnaround, Accelerated growth, Realignment, or Sustaining success? Each demands a fundamentally different strategy.
2. **Resist the action imperative.** The pressure to "do something" immediately is dangerous. Learning *is* your first action.
3. **Have the five conversations with your boss:** Align on (1) the situation diagnosis, (2) expectations for the first 90 days, (3) resources available, (4) communication style, (5) your development.
4. **Check your box** (Arbinger): Are you entering this role trying to prove yourself (Better-Than), feeling entitled (I-Deserve), or needing to appear impressive (Must-Be-Seen-As)? Any of these = [[self-deception]] that will undermine your first 90 days.

### Days 1–30 — Learn Systematically

1. **Create a learning plan.** Identify the 10–15 people who know what you need to know. Schedule one-on-ones.
2. **Ask the same 5 questions to everyone:**
   - What's working well that I should preserve?
   - What's not working that I should address?
   - If you were in my role, what would you do first?
   - What resources are underutilized?
   - What am I likely to miss?
3. **Map the political landscape.** Identify allies, fence-sitters, and resistors. Don't try to convert resistors yet.
4. **Observe the culture.** How are decisions really made? What's rewarded? What's taboo? What's the gap between stated and actual values?

### Days 30–60 — Secure Early Wins

1. **Choose 2–3 early wins** that are visible, meaningful, and aligned with your intended direction. See [[early-wins]].
2. **Apply WRAP** (Heath) to your choice of early wins: Are you seeing enough options? Reality-testing assumptions? Checking that this isn't just about short-term emotional pressure?
3. **Begin team assessment.** Evaluate competence and alignment. Some inherited team members may not be the right fit — but don't rush this.
4. **Build coalitions proactively.** Invest time with fence-sitters; early wins will move them.

### Days 60–90 — Drive Alignment

1. **Make the harder calls** — restructuring, realignment, difficult conversations — with the credibility you've built.
2. **Set up repeatable rhythms** — communication cadences, decision-making processes, team rituals.
3. **Run a premortem** ([[premortem]]): "It's 6 months from now. This role hasn't worked out. What went wrong?"
4. **Set tripwires** ([[tripwires]]): "If [metric] hasn't improved by [date], I'll reassess my approach."
5. **Conduct a formal 90-day review** with your boss.

---

## Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Applying your old playbook.** What worked in your last role may actively harm you here. Diagnose before prescribing.
- **Over-investing in resistors.** They consume energy disproportionally. Focus on movable allies and fence-sitters.
- **Making promises before learning.** Commitments made in Week 1 constrain your options for months.
- **Neglecting personal transition stress.** New roles are exhausting. Build support systems, protect recovery time, maintain perspective.
- **Entering in the box.** If you're trying to prove yourself rather than serve the organization, people will feel it. See [[outward-mindset]].
