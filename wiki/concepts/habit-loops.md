# Habit Loops

**Definition:** The brain's fundamental learning mechanism: **Trigger → Behavior → Reward**. The brain repeats behaviors associated with positive reward value and drops behaviors with negative reward value. This loop drives all habit formation — beneficial habits, addiction, and anxiety alike.

## Why It Matters

Every persistent pattern — good or bad — runs on a habit loop. Anxiety is a habit loop (trigger: uncertainty → behavior: worry → reward: illusion of control). Procrastination is a habit loop. Avoidance is a habit loop. Understanding the mechanism means you can change *any* habit — not through willpower, but by updating the reward value or substituting a bigger, better offer.

## The Loop in Detail

```
Trigger ──→ Behavior ──→ Reward
  ↑                         │
  └─────────────────────────┘
        (reinforcement)
```

The brain doesn't care if the reward is objectively good — it only cares about the *subjective reward value* at the moment. Worry feels productive ("at least I'm doing something"), so the brain reinforces it. Update that value honestly, and the loop weakens.

## Brewer's Three Gears

| Gear | Action | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **1st — Map** | See the trigger → behavior → reward clearly | You can't change what you can't see |
| **2nd — Update** | Ask "How rewarding is this, really?" | Disenchantment beats willpower; the brain drops low-reward behaviors |
| **3rd — BBO** | Replace with curiosity or another genuinely rewarding behavior | The brain needs a better alternative, not a void |
