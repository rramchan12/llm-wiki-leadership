# Maturity Continuum

**Definition:** Covey's three-stage model of human and leadership effectiveness: Dependence → Independence → Interdependence. Growth is not just from incompetent to competent but from "I" to "we" — and interdependence requires more character development than independence.

## Why It Matters

Most self-help and leadership development targets independence — being capable, self-reliant, disciplined. Covey argues this is only the second stage. The highest effectiveness — in organizations, families, and leadership — is interdependent: the capacity to achieve far more through genuine collaboration, synergy, and mutual accountability than any individual could alone.

The mistake: treating independence as the goal. Many high-achieving leaders are very good at independence and terrible at interdependence. Their effectiveness hit a ceiling they don't understand.

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Dependence
**Paradigm:** "You take care of me."

The starting point of all humans. You depend on others for direction, motivation, resources, and results. You succeed or fail based on what others do *for* you or *to* you. This isn't a failing — all development starts here. The problem is leaders who never leave it.

Characteristics: externalize accountability, wait for direction, reactive, blame-prone.

### Stage 2: Independence
**Paradigm:** "I can do it."

The achievement of self-reliance. You can think, act, and deliver without leaning on others. Physical independence (financial self-sufficiency), intellectual independence (forming your own views), emotional independence (not needing others' approval).

This is where most Western self-help resides. The model: discipline → success. And it works, up to a point.

**The ceiling of independence:** At the leadership level, the most important results require other people. The team, the organization, the community — these require interdependence. An independently excellent leader who cannot collaborate, synergize, or draw out others' best will hit a ceiling no amount of personal discipline overcomes.

### Stage 3: Interdependence
**Paradigm:** "We can do it together."

The highest and most mature level. Interdependence is:
- Not dependency with a better name — it requires independence first
- Not compromise (each party gives up something) — it creates synergy (each party gains more than either could alone)
- Built on character: integrity, genuine care, humility, courage

Characteristics: collaborative accountability, seeks synergy, listens to understand, sees others' perspectives as resources not threats, maintains Win/Win orientation.

## What Unlocks Each Stage

| Transition | What's Required |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Dependence → Independence | Self-discipline, proactivity, personal integrity (Habits 1–3) |
| Independence → Interdependence | Genuine care for others, empathic listening, Win/Win orientation (Habits 4–6) |

Habit 7 (Sharpen the Saw) sustains forward movement at all stages.
