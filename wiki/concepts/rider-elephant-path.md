# Rider-Elephant-Path

**Definition:** A three-part model of human change behavior from *Switch* (Chip & Dan Heath). The Rider (rational mind) plans and analyzes; the Elephant (emotional mind) provides energy and motivation; the Path (environment/situation) shapes behavior by default. Sustainable change requires aligning all three.

## Why It Matters

Most change efforts fail because leaders default to Rider-only strategies — data, logic, directives. But the Elephant controls the energy. When the Rider and Elephant disagree, the Elephant wins because willpower is exhaustible. And even when both align, a poorly designed Path creates friction that derails the best intentions. Effective leaders learn to address all three simultaneously.

## The Three Levers

| Lever | Target | Tactics |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Direct the Rider** | Clarity | Find bright spots, script critical moves, point to destination |
| **Motivate the Elephant** | Energy & identity | Find the feeling, shrink the change, grow your people |
| **Shape the Path** | Environment | Tweak environment, build habits (action triggers), rally the herd |
