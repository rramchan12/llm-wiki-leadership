# Numeracy Translation

**Definition:** The deliberate conversion of abstract numbers and statistics into concrete, human-scale comparisons, analogies, and experiences that an audience can viscerally understand and act upon. Coined and systematized by Heath & Starr in *Making Numbers Count* (2022).

## Why It Matters

Data-driven leadership fails not because leaders lack data, but because they present data in the form most convenient for the presenter (raw numbers) rather than the form most usable for the audience (felt experience). The human brain evolved for story, proportion, and sensory experience — not for multi-digit statistics.

Numeracy translation is the highest-leverage communication skill for leaders who rely on data to persuade, motivate, or align: executives presenting strategy, managers justifying resource requests, analysts advising decision-makers, or anyone trying to make an abstract risk feel real.

## The Core Techniques

### 1. Concrete Comparisons
Anchor numbers in physical objects, distances, or durations the audience already knows.
- "A million seconds is 11.5 days. A billion seconds is 31.7 years." [VERIFY]
- Scale to the audience's body, neighborhood, or daily routine.

### 2. Human-Scale Reduction
Reduce large or small aggregates to per-person, per-day, or per-unit frames.
- National funding gaps → dollars per student per year
- Corporate carbon output → grams per product, per mile

### 3. The One-Person Rule (Emotional Concreteness)
Statistics numb; individuals move. Zoom from the statistic to one specific person experiencing it.
- Not "3.2 million uninsured workers" — "Maria, 34, a nurse in Cleveland who hasn't seen a doctor herself in six years."
- Related to Kotler's "moral beauty" and Heath's own Emotional principle in [[sticky-communication]].

### 4. Proportion Over Absolute
Humans grasp relative magnitude far better than absolute numbers.
- "This represents 1 in 4 customers" > "23.4% of the base"
- Pie-slice framing, body-scale analogies, time analogies.

### 5. Experiential Translation
Convert numbers into mini-scenarios the audience can mentally simulate — a brief narrative that makes the quantity feel lived.

### 6. Rounding and Precision Discipline
Counterintuitively, adding more decimal places often reduces persuasion. Round to the level of precision the audience can act on; beyond that, specificity obscures rather than clarifies.

## Relationship to SUCCESs

Numeracy translation is the deep extension of the **Concrete** and **Emotional** tracks of Heath's own [[sticky-communication]] framework. The parent framework says "be concrete"; this book is the comprehensive how-to manual for doing so with quantitative data.

| SUCCESs Principle | Numeracy Translation Technique |
|---|---|
| Concrete | Comparisons, analogies, human-scale reduction |
| Emotional | One-person rule, experiential scenarios |
| Unexpected | Lead with a counterintuitive number to create a curiosity gap |
| Simple | Round aggressively; eliminate precision beyond what is actionable |
