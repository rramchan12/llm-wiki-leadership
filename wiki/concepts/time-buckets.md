# Time Buckets

**Definition:** Bill Perkins's planning framework for allocating experiences to life stages. Divide your anticipated life into roughly 5–10 year "buckets" and ask, for each bucket: what experiences are uniquely enabled, or much better, during this stage? Then deliberately fund and schedule them — before the window closes.

## Why It Matters

Without intentional time-bucket planning, we tend to defer all experiences to a future "when things settle down," while particular windows close permanently. Physical capability, social context (young children, aging parents, professional peers), and appetite for certain experience types all change with life stage. **The right experience at the wrong time is a lesser experience; many experiences are only right within specific windows.**

## Bucket Structure (Illustrative)

| Life Stage | What's Uniquely Possible | Common Deferrals to Avoid |
|------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 20s | Physical extremes, backpacking, building foundational relationships | "I'll do it when I have more money" |
| 30s | Adventure with energy and more resources, starting a family, career acceleration | "I'll travel when the kids are grown" |
| 40s | Experiences with children at optimal ages, peak career impact, moderate adventure | "I'll go deeper with family next year" |
| 50s | Experiences requiring sustained energy before mobility declines; giving while living | "I'll spend more when I retire" |
| 60s | Active retirement; moderate travel; giving to next generation while relationships are strong | "I'll do that later" |
| 70s+ | Reflective pleasures; relationships; witnessing outcomes of gifts given | - |

## The Mismatch Problem

Perkins observes a structural problem: most people's **wealth curve** (net worth rising over career) is out of phase with their **health/energy curve** (declining from mid-life). By the time they have enough money to do what they dreamed, they can't do it as fully. Time buckets resolve this by front-loading experiences to their optimal life stage rather than waiting for financial sufficiency.
