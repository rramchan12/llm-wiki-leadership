# Sticky Communication (SUCCESs)

**Definition:** A framework from *Made to Stick* for making ideas memorable and actionable. Six principles — Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories — counter the Curse of Knowledge and ensure an idea survives transmission.

## Why It Matters

Leaders constantly need ideas to land and persist: strategy communication, feedback, change narratives, team vision. Most fail because the communicator is cursed with knowledge — they can't remember what it's like not to know. SUCCESs provides a systematic antidote.

## The Six Principles

1. **Simple:** Find the core. If your team remembers only one thing, what must it be? (Commander's Intent.)
2. **Unexpected:** Break patterns to grab attention. Use curiosity gaps to hold it.
3. **Concrete:** Sensory, tangible, specific. "Names, dates, and places" not "synergize our core competencies."
4. **Credible:** Authority, vivid details, testable claims. The Sinatra Test.
5. **Emotional:** Make them feel it. Appeal to identity, not just self-interest. One person, not statistics.
6. **Stories:** Simulation + inspiration. Three core plots: Challenge, Connection, Creativity.
