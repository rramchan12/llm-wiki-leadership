# Stoic Virtues

**Definition:** The four cardinal virtues in Stoic leadership practice: justice, courage, moderation, and wisdom. They act as a practical quality gate for decisions, communication, and conduct.

## Why It Matters

Technical competence without character becomes brittle under stress. The virtue lens gives leaders a repeatable way to check whether a decision is merely expedient or genuinely sound.

## The Four Virtues in Practice

- **Justice:** Is this fair to stakeholders, including those with less power?
- **Courage:** Am I avoiding this because it is uncomfortable, or is there a principled reason to wait?
- **Moderation:** Am I overreacting, overcontrolling, or escalating unnecessarily?
- **Wisdom:** Does this decision hold up when I consider second-order effects?
