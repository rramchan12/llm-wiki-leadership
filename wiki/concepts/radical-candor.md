# Radical Candor

**Definition:** A feedback philosophy that combines Caring Personally (seeing employees as whole humans with lives, aspirations, and feelings) with Challenging Directly (telling people clearly when their work isn't good enough). The 2×2 grid — Care Personally × Challenge Directly — maps four quadrants of management behavior.

## Why It Matters

Most managers default to Ruinous Empathy: they care about their people but avoid the discomfort of honest feedback. The result is that people don't grow, problems fester, and resentment builds. Radical Candor provides a simple, memorable compass that recalibrates every interaction — from praise to performance reviews to difficult terminations.

## The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Care | Challenge | Result |
|----------|------|-----------|--------|
| **Radical Candor** | High | High | Growth, trust, performance |
| **Ruinous Empathy** | High | Low | Stagnation, hidden problems |
| **Obnoxious Aggression** | Low | High | Compliance, fear, attrition |
| **Manipulative Insincerity** | Low | Low | Politics, disengagement |
