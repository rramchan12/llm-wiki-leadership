# Goal Setting

**Definition:** The deliberate practice of setting clear, challenging objectives to direct focus, filter reality, and boost motivation. Grounded in Locke & Latham's research showing properly set goals increase motivation by ~25%.

## Why It Matters

Goals and fears are the brain's primary filters on reality. Without clear goals, attention scatters. With them, the brain amplifies signals that support the objective and dampens distractions. Humans are visually oriented, goal-directed creatures — hardwired to go where they look.

## Key Principles

- **Locke & Latham's research:** Specific, challenging goals boost motivation ~25% over vague or easy goals
- **Goals as filters:** Your brain highlights information that supports your goals and suppresses what doesn't
- **Visual orientation:** Humans go where they look. Stare at the target with "excited curiosity"
- **Fear vs. goals:** If fear is your filter, you see threats. If goals are your filter, you see opportunities.
- **Track progress:** Performance journals make invisible gains visible and sustain motivation
