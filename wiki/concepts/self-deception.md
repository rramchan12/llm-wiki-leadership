# Self-Deception

**Definition:** A state of blindness to your own role in creating the problems you're experiencing — particularly in relationships and leadership. You see others' behavior clearly while being unable to see how your own behavior is contributing to (or causing) the very dynamics you resent. Arbinger calls this "being in the box."

## Why It Matters

Self-deception is the root cause of most persistent interpersonal problems. When you're self-deceived, you don't know you're self-deceived — that's the trap. You see yourself as reasonable and justified, and others as the problem. Every leadership failure, every toxic team, every stuck relationship has self-deception as a contributing factor. You cannot fix what you cannot see.

## The Self-Betrayal → Box Sequence

1. You sense something you should do for another person (help, listen, acknowledge)
2. You choose not to do it (**self-betrayal**)
3. You need to justify your choice → you inflate your importance and their faults
4. You now see the world through a distorted lens (**the box**)
5. You provoke others to behave in ways that confirm your distorted view
6. They enter their own box toward you → **collusion**

## The Four Boxes (from Anatomy of Peace)

| Box | Self-Image | View of Others |
|---|---|---|
| **Better-Than** | Superior, important | Inferior, incapable |
| **I-Deserve** | Entitled, unappreciated | Ungrateful, they owe me |
| **Must-Be-Seen-As** | Need to appear a certain way | Threats to my image |
| **Worse-Than** | Not enough, broken | Better than me, have it together |
