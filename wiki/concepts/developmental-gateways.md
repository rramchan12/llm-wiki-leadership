# Developmental Gateways

**Definition:** Critical skill thresholds that, once crossed, unlock qualitatively new levels of performance. Progress in Kotler's model is not linear — it happens in jumps when key gateway skills are mastered.

## Why It Matters

Most people experience performance plateaus and interpret them as hitting a ceiling. Developmental gateways reframe plateaus: they signal that a specific bottleneck skill hasn't yet been consolidated. Until that gateway skill is solid, additional practice on other skills yields diminishing returns. Identifying and targeting the current gateway is the highest-leverage use of deliberate practice time.

## How Books Treat This

| Book | Stance | Key Insight |
|------|--------|-------------|
| [[kotler-gnar-country]] | Defines | Kotler identifies specific developmental gateways across the lifespan and within each domain of mastery. Key life-stage gateways: identity resolution (~30), intrinsic alignment (~40), emotional clearing (~50), counteracting risk aversion (ongoing). Domain gateways vary by field but follow the same pattern: master the bottleneck, unlock the next level. |

## Key Principles

- **Non-linear progress:** Skill acquisition jumps, not slides — gateways explain why
- **Bottleneck targeting:** Identify the current gateway skill and concentrate deliberate practice there
- **Life-stage gateways:** Identity resolution (30s), intrinsic alignment (40s), emotional clearing (50s)
- **Diminishing returns elsewhere:** Balanced training that avoids the bottleneck is less efficient than targeted gateway work
