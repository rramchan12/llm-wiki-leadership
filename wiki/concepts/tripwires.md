# Tripwires

**Definition:** Pre-set conditions — a threshold, deadline, or metric — that automatically trigger a decision review. A tripwire prevents sunk-cost escalation by forcing a deliberate re-evaluation before more resources are committed.

---

## Why It Matters

The natural human tendency is to continue investing in a path once chosen, especially as costs mount. This is sunk-cost bias — throwing good money (or time, or energy) after bad because we can't bear to "waste" what's already been spent. Tripwires counter this by creating a *commitment device*: you decide in advance, while rational, to stop and reassess if specific conditions arise.

Tripwires work because:
- They're set **before** emotions are engaged — before sunk costs accumulate
- They shift the question from "should we keep going?" (hard to answer) to "have we hit the tripwire?" (easy to verify)
- They make stopping feel like **following the plan**, not admitting failure

---

## Types of Tripwires

### Threshold-based
Set a concrete threshold that triggers review.
- "If our customer acquisition cost exceeds $X, we pause and reassess."
- "If team morale scores fall below Y two quarters in a row, we restructure the team."
- Warren Buffett's variant: "If in doubt, don't." — a qualitative tripwire.

### Time-based
Set a deadline for a mandatory check-in.
- "In six months, we will evaluate whether this initiative is on track."
- "By Q3, we need to see X metric or we exit the market."

### Milestone-based
Tie reviews to project stages, not calendars.
- "Before we proceed to Phase 3, we must have evidence of X."
- Stage-gate processes in product development are a formalized version of tripwires.

---

## How to Set a Good Tripwire

1. **Make it specific and measurable.** "If things aren't going well" is not a tripwire. "If revenue hasn't reached $500K by month 18" is.
2. **Set it at the moment of commitment,** while you're still emotionally neutral.
3. **Assign someone to monitor it.** Tripwires without owners are ignored.
4. **Distinguish tripwire (stop and reassess) from kill switch (automatic exit).** Not every tripwire leads to stopping — it leads to a deliberate choice.

---

## Relationship to Premortem

The [[premortem]] identifies what could go wrong. Tripwires are the early-warning system that detects when those things are actually happening. Together:
- Premortem: *What would failure look like?*
- Tripwire: *When we start to see [sign of failure], we stop and reassess.*
