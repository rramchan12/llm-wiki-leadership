# Working Backwards / PR-FAQ Method

**Definition:** A product and strategy development technique pioneered at Amazon in which every new initiative begins with a mock press release (PR) written from the customer's perspective — before any engineering, design, or planning work begins. The press release is paired with a Frequently Asked Questions document (FAQ) that surfaces hard questions early. Together they force clarity on the customer value proposition before resources are committed.

## Why It Matters

Most organizations start product development from the inside — "what can we build?" — and then discover too late whether customers actually want it. Working Backwards inverts this sequence: you begin with a vivid, concrete description of the customer experience you want to create and only then determine what it takes to build it.

The PR-FAQ is a forcing function, not a formality. If you cannot write a compelling one-page press release describing the customer benefit in plain language, you do not yet have a clear vision. The discipline of writing it surfaces the gaps before they become expensive.

## The PR-FAQ Structure

### The Press Release (≈1 page)
Written as if the product has already launched, dated to the anticipated launch date.

**Required elements:**
1. **Headline:** What is this? (Product name + one-line description)
2. **Subheadline:** Who is the customer? What is the benefit?
3. **Geography/date:** Where/when is it launching?
4. **Opening paragraph:** Describes the customer's problem and how the product solves it
5. **Customer quote:** A realistic quote from a customer explaining why it matters to them
6. **How it works:** A brief, non-technical description of the experience
7. **Internal executive quote:** How this fits the company's mission
8. **Call to action:** Where to learn more

Rules: No jargon. No internal acronyms. No passive voice. Read it aloud — if it doesn't sound like a real press release, it isn't.

### The FAQ (2–5 pages)
Two sections:
- **Customer FAQ:** What questions would a first-time customer ask? Answer them honestly. If the answers are bad, the product isn't ready to pitch.
- **Internal FAQ:** What will leadership, legal, finance, operations ask? Resource requirements, dependencies, biggest risks, competitive landscape.

---

## Why PR-FAQ Over Traditional Business Cases
| Business Case | PR-FAQ |
|---|---|
| Starts from capabilities and financials | Starts from customer experience |
| Often written to justify a decision already made | Designed to surface problems before commitment |
| PowerPoint/bullets hide logic gaps | Prose narrative requires coherent thinking |
| Internal language | Customer language — the Curse of Knowledge antidote |

---

## Working Backwards as Organizational Reflex

The method extends beyond product launches:
- **Process design:** What would the customer of this process say in a press release praising it?
- **Strategy presentations:** Force the "so what for the customer" before the financial model
- **Hiring:** What does the team's work look like from the perspective of the person it serves?
