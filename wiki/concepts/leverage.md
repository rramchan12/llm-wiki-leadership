# Leverage

**Definition:** Naval Ravikant's framework for force multipliers on your productive effort — ways to produce output that is not linearly proportional to your time. The goal is to **decouple income and impact from hours worked**, allowing your effort to compound rather than cap at the number of hours you can sell.

## Why It Matters

Without leverage, you are selling time. Time is finite and non-compounding; once sold, it's gone. With leverage, you create or invest in systems that work while you sleep. This is the structural difference between a consultant (sells hours) and a software founder (code runs regardless of whether they are awake). Leverage explains why wealth creation is possible at scale for individuals; it is not simply about "working harder."

## The Four Types of Leverage

| Type | Description | Access Barrier | Key Property |
|------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Labor** | People working for or with you | Trust; management skill | Oldest form; requires human coordination |
| **Capital** | Money working for you (investments, equity) | Track record, network, or luck | Scales well but requires significant capital first |
| **Code** | Software that runs without ongoing input | Technical skill | Zero marginal cost of replication — Naval's most praised lever |
| **Media/Content** | Writing, podcast, video, teaching at scale | Creativity, distribution | No marginal cost; ideas replicate freely |

**Code and media are the democratizing levers:** they require no permission, no capital base, and scale without marginal cost. A blog post, a YouTube video, or an open-source library can reach millions with minimal incremental effort.

## Productize Yourself

Naval's synthesis formula:

> **Specific knowledge** + **Accountability** + **Leverage** = **Wealth**

"Productize yourself" means:
1. Identify your [[specific-knowledge]]
2. Build accountability (put your name and reputation on work)
3. Add leverage (code, media, capital, or labor multipliers)
4. Charge market rates for the outcome — not for your time

## Permission vs. Permission-less Leverage

- **Labor and capital** require others' trust or resources — they need permission
- **Code and media** require only skills and effort — they are permission-less

The implication for early career: build code and media leverage before you have capital or management authority. The barrier to entry is discipline and skill, not credentials or resources.

## Relationship to Adjacent Concepts

- **[[specific-knowledge]]:** The *what* your leverage multiplies. Leverage without specific knowledge is generic; with it, leverage is defensible and durable.
- **[[intrinsic-motivation]]:** Motivation stack that sustains the long effort required to build real leverage (especially code or media, which take years to compound)
- **[[compounding]] / long-term thinking:** Leverage compounds — a piece of content or software continues generating value long after it's built
- **[[ruthless-prioritization]]:** To build an asset with leverage, you must protect the time to build it — requires aggressive prioritization
