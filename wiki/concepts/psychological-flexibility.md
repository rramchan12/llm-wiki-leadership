# Psychological Flexibility

**Definition:** The ability to be present, open up to difficult internal experiences (thoughts, feelings, sensations), and take effective action guided by values — even when it's uncomfortable. The central target of ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy).

## Why It Matters

Psychological rigidity — fusion with thoughts, avoidance of discomfort, disconnection from values — is the common factor in most mental health struggles. Flexibility is the antidote. A psychologically flexible person doesn't need to feel good to do good. They can hold discomfort while still moving toward what matters. This is the inner game behind every other skill in this wiki.

## The Six Pillars (ACT Model)

| Pillar | Flexibility | Rigidity |
|---|---|---|
| **Defusion** | Thoughts are mental events, not commands | Thoughts are literally true |
| **Acceptance** | Willingness to have uncomfortable feelings | Fighting, avoiding, suppressing feelings |
| **Present moment** | Flexible attention to now | Lost in past regrets or future fears |
| **Self-as-context** | "I am the observer of my experience" | "I am my anxiety / my failure" |
| **Values** | Clear on what matters; values guide action | Disconnected; actions driven by avoidance |
| **Committed action** | Doing what matters despite discomfort | Inaction, impulsivity, or avoidance patterns |
