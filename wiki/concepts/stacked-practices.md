# Stacked Practices

**Definition:** Designing activities that solve multiple training/growth problems simultaneously, integrating practice into existing routines rather than adding separate time blocks.

## Why It Matters

Time is the scarcest resource, especially when pursuing ambitious goals alongside work and family. Stacked practices eliminate the "I don't have time" objection by embedding training into what you already do. The result: consistent, compounding progress with no schedule disruption.

## How Books Treat This

| Book | Stance | Key Insight |
|------|--------|-------------|
| [[kotler-gnar-country]] | Practical method | Kotler wears a weight vest while hiking his dogs → trains backcountry skiing muscles without changing schedule. Meditation → trains focus AND lowers stress. Every activity serves double duty. |

## Key Principles

- **Multi-tool solutions:** Every activity should solve 2+ problems (physical, cognitive, emotional)
- **No new time blocks:** Overlay training on existing habits (commute, dog walks, chores)
- **Get comfortable with uncomfortable:** Progressive discomfort embedded in daily life builds resilience naturally
- **Compound effect:** Small stacks done daily outperform intensive sporadic sessions
