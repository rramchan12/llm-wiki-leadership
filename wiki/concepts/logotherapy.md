# Logotherapy / Will to Meaning

**Definition:** Frankl's therapeutic framework based on the claim that the primary human motivational force is the search for meaning (*logos* = meaning in Greek), not pleasure (Freud) nor power (Adler). Logotherapy treats suffering and existential emptiness as signal that a person's will to meaning is frustrated — and healing comes through finding or creating meaning, not through symptom elimination alone.

## Why It Matters

Most leadership frameworks treat motivation as reward/incentive management or fear-removal. Logotherapy goes deeper: sustained motivation, especially through hardship, requires a felt sense of *why the work matters*. When that "why" is strong, nearly any "how" becomes bearable. When it collapses, even comfortable lives feel hollow.

The leadership implication: you cannot sustain a team through prolonged difficulty by offering bonuses and removing friction alone. You must connect people to meaning — in their work, in the mission, in each other.

## The Three Paths to Meaning

| Path | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Creative values** | What you give — through work, creation, deeds | Writing a book; building a product; teaching |
| **Experiential values** | What you receive — love, beauty, truth, relationship | Loving a person; witnessing something transcendent |
| **Attitudinal values** | How you face unavoidable suffering | Bearing illness with courage; choosing dignity in crisis |

**Attitudinal values are the most radical**: they are available even when all else is stripped away — as Frankl found in Auschwitz.

## Key Ideas

- **Existential vacuum:** The felt sense that life lacks meaning; manifests as boredom, depression, aggression. Frankl diagnosed this as epidemic in affluent modern societies where traditional meaning structures (religion, prescribed roles) have weakened.
- **Noögenic neurosis:** Psychological distress arising not from psychodynamic conflict but from meaninglessness itself. Standard therapy may miss it entirely.
- **The last human freedom:** Between stimulus and response there is a space — and in that space lies the freedom to choose one's response. This is the ultimate sovereignty: it cannot be taken by circumstance.
- **Tragic optimism:** [[tragic-optimism]] — saying yes to life despite the tragic triad of pain, guilt, and transience.
