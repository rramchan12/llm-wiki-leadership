# Values-Based Action

**Definition:** Taking action guided by what truly matters to you (your values) rather than by what feels comfortable, what avoids pain, or what others expect. Values are chosen directions, not destinations — you never "arrive" at a value.

## Why It Matters

Without values clarity, decisions default to emotional avoidance: we do what feels safe, not what matters. With values clarity, you have an internal compass that works even when emotions are screaming. Values-based action is the engine of both ACT (Harris) and Kotler's motivation stack — and it's the difference between a meaningful life and a comfortable but hollow one.

## Values ≠ Goals

| Values | Goals |
|---|---|
| Directions (like "north") | Destinations (like "New York") |
| Never completed | Can be achieved or failed |
| Intrinsic and chosen | Often externally imposed |
| Guide ongoing behavior | Motivate specific actions |
| Example: "Being a caring partner" | Example: "Plan a date night this Friday" |
