# Defining Moments

**Definition:** Experiences that are disproportionately memorable and meaningful — shaped by Elevation, Insight, Pride, and Connection. They occur naturally at peaks, pits, and transitions, but leaders can design them deliberately.

## Why It Matters

Memory and meaning don't track the average — they track the peaks. A single well-designed moment (a first day, a recognition ceremony, a shared challenge) can redefine how an employee feels about their role, a customer feels about a brand, or a team feels about each other. Leaders who manage only the average miss the highest-leverage opportunity for impact.

## The Four Elements

| Element | Creates | Design Principle |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **Elevation** | Transcendence, delight | Break the script; add sensory richness; raise stakes |
| **Insight** | Perspective shift, "aha" | Structure "tripping over the truth" — let people discover it themselves |
| **Pride** | Achievement and recognition | Mark milestones; recognize effort, not just outcomes; make progress visible |
| **Connection** | Bond, belonging | Create shared struggle, mutual vulnerability, synchronized experience |
