# Cognitive Distortions

**Definition:** Systematic errors in thinking — patterns where the mind consistently misreads reality in predictable, self-defeating ways. First cataloged by Aaron Beck and popularized by David Burns as the 10 distortions.

## Why It Matters

Cognitive distortions are the invisible architecture of bad moods, bad decisions, and broken relationships. They feel like truth — "I *feel* it, so it must be real" — but they don't survive scrutiny. Recognizing them is the first step to freedom: whether through Burns's CBT (challenge the content), Harris's ACT (change your relationship to it), or Brewer's habit-loop approach (update the reward value).

## The 10 Distortions (Burns)

| # | Distortion | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | All-or-nothing thinking | Black/white, no middle ground |
| 2 | Overgeneralization | One event → universal rule |
| 3 | Mental filter | Fixate on the negative, ignore the positive |
| 4 | Disqualifying the positive | Good things "don't count" |
| 5 | Jumping to conclusions | Mind reading or fortune telling |
| 6 | Magnification/minimization | Catastrophize bad, shrink good |
| 7 | Emotional reasoning | "I feel it, therefore it's true" |
| 8 | Should statements | Rigid rules for self and others |
| 9 | Labeling | Global label from one event |
| 10 | Personalization | Everything is your fault |

## Three Approaches to Distortions

| Approach | Method | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **CBT (Burns)** | Challenge content: "Is this thought accurate?" | Acute depression; identifiable distorted thoughts |
| **ACT (Harris)** | Defuse from thought: "I'm having the thought that…" | Avoidance-driven anxiety; rumination loops |
| **Habit-loop (Brewer)** | Update reward value: "Is worrying helping?" | Chronic anxiety; entrenched thought habits |

These three approaches are complementary, not competing.

## CBT vs. ACT: A Note on Complementary Philosophies
Burns (CBT) holds that distorted thinking *causes* emotional distress — fixing the thought fixes the feeling. Harris (ACT) reframes the problem: it is not the thought content but the *fusion* with thought (believing thoughts are literally true) that causes suffering. ACT does not require thoughts to be "corrected" — only defused from. In practice: use **cognitive restructuring** (Burns) when you need to evaluate the accuracy of a belief; use **cognitive defusion** (Harris) when the belief's accuracy is irrelevant and you need to reduce its behavioral grip. The two tools target different levers and work best in combination.
