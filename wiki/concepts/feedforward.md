# Feedforward

**Definition:** A future-focused alternative to traditional feedback: instead of asking what someone did wrong in the past, ask for two specific suggestions to help you do better in the future. The past cannot be changed; the future can.

## Why It Matters

Traditional feedback is retrospective — it analyzes what already happened. This creates predictable problems:
- The recipient gets defensive (the past is fixed and they can't un-do it)
- The conversation becomes about assigning blame rather than enabling change
- The relationship bears the cost of the emotional friction

Feedforward eliminates these costs entirely by orienting to what can actually be changed: the future. It is dramatically more actionable, more positive, and less threatening — for both parties.

## The Feedforward Exercise (Goldsmith)

**Protocol:**
1. Pick one behavior you want to improve (e.g., "I want to be a better listener")
2. Approach a colleague and share the goal
3. Ask: *"Can you give me two suggestions I could use to become a better listener in the future?"*
4. Listen to both suggestions
5. Say: "Thank you." Do not debate, dismiss, or qualify
6. Move to the next person and repeat

**Rules for the recipient:**
- No defending the past ("I only interrupted because...")
- No dismissing suggestions ("That won't work for me because...")
- No explaining yourself
- Just: "Thank you"

**Why "thank you" matters:** Saying thank you — rather than responding, disagreeing, or discussing — trains the habit of receiving input without defensiveness. This habit transfer to the actual work itself.

## Contrast with Traditional Feedback

| Traditional Feedback | Feedforward |
|---------------------|------------|
| Past-focused | Future-focused |
| "Here's what you did wrong" | "Here's what you could do better" |
| Fixed; can't be changed | Actionable; can be implemented tomorrow |
| Triggers defensiveness | Invites optimism |
| About the relationship with history | About the possibility of change |
| Feels like judgment | Feels like coaching |

## How Books Treat This

| Book | Stance | Key Insight |
|------|--------|-------------|
| [[goldsmith-what-got-you-here]] | Defines and advocates | Feedforward as both a personal development tool and a cultural norm; replacing the 360 feedback culture with a forward-looking alternative |
