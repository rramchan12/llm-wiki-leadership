# Fear Management

**Definition:** The deliberate practice of identifying, tolerating, and ultimately using fear as information rather than allowing it to drive avoidance or shutdown.

## Why It Matters

Fear is not the enemy of high performance — unmanaged fear is. When fear triggers avoidance, it shrinks your skill set and keeps you away from the challenge levels where flow and growth occur. When fear is managed well, it becomes a signal pointing toward the edge of your current ability — exactly where progress happens. Leaders and performers who develop this skill can sustain growth in domains where others retreat.

## Key Principles

- **Fear as signal:** Fear near the edge of your skill set is information, not a stop sign
- **The 10% rule:** Never increase challenge by more than 10% at a time — keeps fear in the productive zone
- **Incremental exposure:** Progressive overload applied to fear: repeated small doses build tolerance
- **Fear vs. paralysis:** Manageable fear produces focus and heightened awareness; unmanaged fear produces freeze or flight
