# Psychological Safety

**Definition:** The shared belief within a team that it is safe to speak up — to raise problems, admit errors, offer unpopular opinions, ask questions, and take interpersonal risks — without fear of punishment or humiliation.

*(The term was coined and empirically studied by Amy Edmondson at Harvard; Schein's and DeMarco's work directly supports and anticipates it.)*

## Why It Matters

The most common cause of preventable organizational disasters is not ignorance — it's silence. The person who knew about the o-ring problem, the medication error, the strategic miscalculation was usually present. They didn't speak because the cost of speaking (embarrassment, retaliation, being labeled a troublemaker) exceeded the perceived benefit.

Amy Edmondson's landmark research found that psychological safety is the primary predictor of team performance in knowledge work — more than average IQ, more than technical skill, more than team size. Google's Project Aristotle replicated this finding.

## What Creates Psychological Safety

| Behavior | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Humble Inquiry** (Schein) | Leader models vulnerability; signals it's safe to not-know |
| **Acknowledging errors** | Leaders who admit mistakes lower the perceived cost of error admission |
| **Genuine listening** | People speak up when they believe their input will be heard |
| **Not shooting the messenger** | The #1 destroyer of safety; one punished messenger trains everyone else into silence |
| **Framing work as learning** | "We're figuring this out together" removes the threat of looking incompetent |
| **Consistent follow-through** | Promised action that never materializes trains people that speaking up is pointless |

## What Destroys Psychological Safety

- **Public humiliation** — especially from senior leaders
- **Punishing bad news** — reacting to unwelcome information with anger or blame
- **Defensiveness when challenged** — the message: "never challenge me again"
- **Status-based communication norms** — "that's above your pay grade"
- **Incentivizing silence** — rewarding teams for "no problems" (which means problems are hidden)
- **Zero-sum competition** — team members who compete for reviews won't surface shared problems

## Psychological Safety vs. Comfort

A common misunderstanding: psychological safety does not mean avoiding difficult conversations. It means the opposite:

| Safety | Comfort |
|--------|---------|
| "I can say what I actually think" | "We never disagree" |
| Conflict is surfaced and addressed | Conflict is avoided |
| Mistakes are reported and learned from | Mistakes are hidden |
| High-performance accountability | Low-accountability niceness |

**Psychological safety is the prerequisite for candor, not its substitute.** (Cf. [[radical-candor]], [[team-jell]])
