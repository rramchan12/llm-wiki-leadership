# The Stoic Leader — John Sellars & Justin Stead (2022)

**One-liner:** Leadership quality comes less from controlling outcomes and more from mastering judgment, character, and action under pressure.

## Core Thesis

Stoicism is a practical operating system for leaders: separate what is controllable from what is not, choose responses aligned with virtue, and treat adversity as training material rather than a reason for panic or blame.

## Key Frameworks

1. **[[dichotomy-of-control]]** — Focus attention and effort on your judgments, choices, and behavior, not on external volatility.
2. **[[stoic-virtues]]** — Justice, courage, moderation, and wisdom as a decision-quality checklist.
3. **Premeditation of adversity** — Rehearse likely setbacks ahead of time to improve calm response; closely related to [[premortem]].
4. **View from above** — Zoom out from ego and immediacy to assess broader system effects.
5. **Present-moment execution** — Regret and anxiety are poor decision partners; act on what can be done now.
6. **Character as compounding habit** — Repeated small responses under stress shape long-run leadership reliability.
