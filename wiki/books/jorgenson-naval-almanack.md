# The Almanack of Naval Ravikant — Eric Jorgenson (2020)

> **LLM Confidence: HIGH** — This is a well-documented compilation of Naval Ravikant's public statements, podcast transcripts, and tweets. Detailed knowledge of all core frameworks.

**One-liner:** Build wealth through specific knowledge and leverage, not traded time; build happiness through internal practice, not external achievement — and recognize that the two projects are compatible.

## Core Thesis

Eric Jorgenson curated Naval Ravikant's published wisdom on two parallel tracks: **how to get rich** (without getting lucky or hurting others) and **how to be happy**. Naval's insight is that most people conflate wealth with income (trading time for money, which scales poorly) and confuse happiness with achievement or pleasure (both of which adapt away). The real targets are **equity and ownership** on the wealth side, and **equanimity through internal work** on the happiness side. Both require long-term thinking, compounding, and escape from the hedonic treadmill.

## Key Frameworks

### 1. [[specific-knowledge]]
The wealth-creation edge that cannot be competed away. Specific knowledge is:
- Found at the intersection of **your genuine curiosity, passion, and skill** — not where market demand is highest
- **Not teachable via formal training**: it emerges from following your nature relentlessly
- Often appears playful to you, hard work to others
- Highly creative or technical; difficult to outsource or automate

> "Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else and replace you."

### 2. [[leverage]]
Four types of leverage that multiply your labor without proportional time cost:
1. **Labor** — managing other people (oldest form; requires trust and management)
2. **Capital** — money working for you (requires luck, trust, or track record to acquire)
3. **Code** — software replicates with zero marginal cost `[Naval's most praised lever]`
4. **Media/Content** — writing, podcasts, video that scale ideas without your presence

The key shift: **"productize yourself"** — find your specific knowledge, add leverage, charge for the output not the time. Code and media have no marginal costs of replication, making them the most powerful.

### 3. Four Types of Luck
1. **Blind luck** — random; happens to you
2. **Luck from hustle** — you create luck by doing more things
3. **Luck from skill** — you notice opportunities others miss because of your expertise
4. **Luck from character** — opportunities seek you out because of your reputation and who you are; *this is the goal*

Building specific knowledge compounds into type 3 and type 4 luck.

### 4. [[happiness-as-skill]]
Happiness is not found in achievement or pleasure — both adapt quickly (hedonic treadmill). Naval's framework:

- **Remove suffering, not pursue pleasure** — most unhappiness is from monkey-mind desire loops; learn to quiet them
- **Presence** — anxiety is about the future; depression is about the past; happiness lives in the present moment
- **Judgment over rules** — develop enough clarity that you can reason from first principles in any situation, rather than following scripts
- **Meditation as debugging** — Naval treats meditation as examining and clearing the mind's code, not as mysticism

> "The most important skill for getting rich is becoming a perpetual learner."
> "Happiness is a choice and a skill."

### 5. Long-Term Thinking and Compounding
- Compound in **relationships**: only work with people you enjoy and trust for the long run
- Compound in **knowledge**: read foundational texts, not trend pieces
- Compound in **reputation**: be scrupulously honest so your reputation precedes you as type-4 luck
- The key: **pick long-term games with long-term people** — most of life's gains come from compounding, not from clever moves

### 6. Specific Knowledge Stacks as Personal Moat
Combining 2–3 skills where you are in the top 25% each creates rare combinations that become very hard to replicate. Naval builds on Scott Adams's concept: being excellent at writing, technical understanding, and persuasion simultaneously is rarer than being the best at one.

### 7. Ethics and "Not Getting Hurt"
Naval adds an important constraint: the goal is to get rich **without harming others** — through value creation, not extraction. Zero-sum games (status, politics) are traps; positive-sum games (wealth creation, knowledge-sharing) compound.
