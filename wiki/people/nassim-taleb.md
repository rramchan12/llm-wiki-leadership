# Nassim Nicholas Taleb

**Role:** Author, former quantitative trader, and Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering at NYU Tandon School of Engineering. Author of the *Incerto* series: *Fooled by Randomness* (2001), *The Black Swan* (2007), *Antifragile* (2012), *Skin in the Game* (2018), *The Bed of Procrustes* (aphorisms). One of the most widely cited original thinkers on risk, uncertainty, and decision-making under uncertainty.

---

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Taleb provides the most rigorous treatment of *asymmetric risk* and *system design under uncertainty* available in popular nonfiction. For leaders, his contribution is a corrective to frameworks that assume predictability: the WRAP, STARS, and five-dysfunctions frameworks are all most powerful in moderate-volatility environments. Taleb tells you what to do when the environment is genuinely unpredictable — which it often is.

His work is also the theoretical foundation for portfolio-style career design, experimental strategy, and the structural rationale for why "skin in the game" (accountability) is not just ethical but epistemically necessary.

## Key Contributions

- **The Black Swan** — Rare, unpredictable, high-impact events dominate actual distributions; conventional risk management ignores them
- **Antifragility** — The property of gaining from disorder; the triad of fragile/robust/antifragile. See [[antifragility]].
- **Optionality and Barbell Strategy** — Asymmetric exposure; large safe core + small high-upside bets, avoid medium risk. See [[optionality-and-asymmetric-bets]].
- **Via Negativa** — Subtract sources of fragility rather than add robustness on top
- **Skin in the Game** — Decision-makers must bear personal consequences; without this, risk is systematically mispriced
- **Lindy Effect** — The longer something has survived, the longer it is likely to survive
