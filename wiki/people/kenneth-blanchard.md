# Kenneth Blanchard

**Role:** Management author, speaker, and consultant; creator of Situational Leadership® and One Minute Management.

## Who He Is

Kenneth H. Blanchard is an American author and management expert. He holds a PhD in administration and management from Cornell University and co-founded The Ken Blanchard Companies, a global leadership development and training organization.

Blanchard has co-written over 60 books — with over 21 million copies sold globally. He is known for making management concepts highly accessible through the parable format: quick, readable, character-driven books that distill complex management insights into actionable simplicity.

## Key Works

- **The One Minute Manager** (1982, with Spencer Johnson) — perhaps the most widely read management book ever; three secrets of effective management
- **Leadership and the One Minute Manager** (1985, with Patricia Zigarmi and Drea Zigarmi) — Situational Leadership®; match management style to development level
- **Raving Fans** (1993, with Sheldon Bowles) — legendary customer service
- **One Minute Mentoring** (2017, with Claire Shankman) — applying the One Minute philosophy to developmental relationships
- **The New One Minute Manager** (2015) — updated edition with One Minute Re-Directs

## Core Ideas

- **One Minute Management:** Goals, Praisings, Reprimands/Redirects (see [[one-minute-management]])
- **Situational Leadership:** Match direction to the employee's development level in the specific task
- **Mentoring Partnership:** Intentional, structured, reciprocal mentoring (see [[mentoring-partnership]])
- **Catch people doing things right:** The foundational reorientation from exception management to positive reinforcement

## Philosophical Connection

Blanchard's work stands on two pillars:
1. **People deserve to know what's expected** — ambiguity about goals is harmful, not empowering
2. **People perform better when they feel good about themselves** — management's job is to create those conditions

Both pillars are humble claims delivered with genuine conviction. Blanchard's longevity, in an era of management fads, reflects the fact that these claims are both simple and true.
