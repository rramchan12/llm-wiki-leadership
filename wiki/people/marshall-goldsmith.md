# Marshall Goldsmith

**Role:** Executive coach, leadership author, professor; widely regarded as one of the world's top executive coaches.

## Who He Is

Marshall Goldsmith is an American executive coach and management educator known for his work on behavioral change in senior leaders. He holds a PhD from UCLA and was named the #1 executive coach in the world by Global Gurus. He has coached hundreds of CEOs and senior leaders at major corporations.

Goldsmith's method distinguishes him from most leadership coaches: rather than focusing on strategy, emotional intelligence, or skill-building, he focuses on the specific **interpersonal behaviors** that hold technically competent leaders back from greater effectiveness.

## Key Works

- **What Got You Here Won't Get You There** (2007) — his signature book; introduces the 20 habits and feedforward
- **Triggers** (2015) — how environmental triggers shape behavior and how to build "active questions" practice
- **The Earned Life** (2022) — leadership, contribution, and fulfillment

## Core Ideas

- **The success delusion:** Past success causes leaders to misattribute current behavior as the cause; makes habits invisible
- **20 habits that hold leaders back:** Behavioral patterns that impede senior effectiveness (see [[behavioral-blind-spots]])
- **Feedforward:** Future-focused alternative to retrospective feedback (see [[feedforward]])
- **Behavioral change process:** Feedback → apology → advertise → listen → follow up

## Relationship to Other Wiki Sources

Goldsmith addresses the **behavioral layer** of leadership failure. Arbinger ([[self-deception]]) addresses the deeper **paradigm layer** — how we see others. Both are necessary: Arbinger shows what's wrong with the foundation; Goldsmith shows what to fix on the surface. Together they describe the full spectrum of interpersonal leadership failure.
