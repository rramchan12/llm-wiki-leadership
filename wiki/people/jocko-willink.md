# Jocko Willink

**Role:** Retired U.S. Navy SEAL commander; led Task Unit Bruiser in the Battle of Ramadi (Iraq, 2006); co-author of *Extreme Ownership* (2015) and founder of Echelon Front leadership consultancy; host of the Jocko Podcast.

---

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Willink represents the most direct application of military leadership principles to organizational leadership in this wiki's library. His "Extreme Ownership" framework is derived from SEAL combat experience — the highest-stakes environment available — and translates into a set of unconditional accountability principles applicable to any organization.

His significance is not rhetorical emphasis on toughness but rather the structural insight: **ownership works because it preserves agency**. It is an evidence-based leadership posture, not a motivational slogan.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Extreme Ownership** — The leader owns all outcomes, unconditionally. See [[extreme-ownership]].
- **Decentralized Command** — Authority pushed to lowest competent level with clear commander's intent. See [[decentralized-command]].
- **Discipline Equals Freedom** — Structured daily discipline creates bandwidth and freedom; disorder creates constant firefighting.
- **No Bad Teams, Only Bad Leaders** — Performance is a leadership variable, not a personnel variable.
