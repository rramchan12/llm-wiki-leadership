# Steven Kotler

**Role:** New York Times bestselling author, journalist, Executive Director of the Flow Research Collective. One of the world's leading experts on human performance and flow states.

## Background

- Author of 14 books (11 bestsellers), including *The Art of Impossible*, *The Rise of Superman*, *Stealing Fire*, *Bold*, *Abundance*
- Work nominated for two Pulitzer Prizes, translated into 50+ languages
- Published in 100+ outlets: NYT Magazine, Wired, Atlantic Monthly, WSJ, TIME, Harvard Business Review
- Co-founder of The Buddy Sue Hospice Home for Old Dogs and Rancho de Chihuahua dog rescue
- Lives in New Mexico with wife, author Joy Nicholson

## Core Philosophy

Kotler's work centers on the idea that peak performance is scientifically trainable — not a gift. Flow states are the mechanism, and they're accessible to anyone willing to structure their environment and practice correctly. In *Gnar Country*, he extends this to aging: the same principles that produce elite performance in young athletes can sustain and grow capability in the second half of life.
