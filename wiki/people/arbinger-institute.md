# The Arbinger Institute

**Role:** Leadership training and consulting organization founded in 1979. Known for its work on self-deception, outward mindset, and conflict resolution. Clients include governments, Fortune 500 companies, and community organizations worldwide. Key intellectual contributor: C. Terry Warner (BYU philosopher).

## Why They Matter to This Wiki

Arbinger provides the deepest treatment in this wiki of *relational* self-awareness — not just how you think (Burns), or how you relate to your thoughts (Harris), but how you *see other people*. Their insight is deceptively simple and profoundly difficult: most interpersonal problems are caused by seeing others as objects rather than people. No technique, skill, or framework can compensate for this foundational failure of perception.

## Key Contributions

- **[[self-deception]]** ("The Box") — Being blind to your own contribution to problems while seeing others' faults clearly.
- **Self-Betrayal** — The origin of the box: sensing you should do something for another person and choosing not to.
- **[[outward-mindset]]** — Seeing others as people with real needs, challenges, and objectives.
- **The Four Boxes** — Better-Than, I-Deserve, Must-Be-Seen-As, Worse-Than. Four flavors of heart-at-war.
- **The Pyramid of Influence** — Get out of the box → build the relationship → listen → teach → correct.
- **Collusion** — Two boxed people provoking each other's worst behavior.
