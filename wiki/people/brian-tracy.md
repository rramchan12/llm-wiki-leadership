# Brian Tracy

**Role:** Self-help author, speaker, and trainer; expert on personal productivity, goal-setting, and sales performance.

## Who He Is

Brian Tracy is a Canadian-American self-help author and professional speaker. He is largely self-educated — having pursued an extensive self-directed reading program after leaving school early — and built a career training salespeople and executives. He has written over 80 books and given seminars in more than 80 countries.

Tracy's work is practical, systematized, and direct. He synthesizes research from psychology, performance science, and business practice into actionable step-by-step frameworks. He is explicit that his ideas are not original research but distillations of the best practices of high performers.

## Key Works

- **Eat That Frog!** (2001) — procrastination management; ABCDE method; 21 techniques
- **Goals!** (2003) — goal-setting framework; 12-step process for achieving any goal
- **The Psychology of Achievement** (audio program, 1980s) — early synthesis of performance psychology
- **The 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership** — note: this title is John Maxwell's; Tracy's leadership work is distinct
- **Maximum Achievement** (1993) — comprehensive success psychology
- **Million Dollar Habits** (2004) — habits of high performers

## Core Ideas

- **Eat the frog:** Do your hardest, most important task first every day (see [[procrastination-framework]])
- **ABCDE method:** Priority ranking by consequence; never work a B while an A exists undone
- **80/20 applied to tasks:** 20% of tasks produce 80% of results; identify and protect that 20%
- **Key Result Areas:** Know the 5–7 outputs your role demands; a weakness in any KRA caps overall performance
- **Continuous learning:** Top performers never stop investing in their own development
- **Clear written goals:** Written goals with deadlines are dramatically more likely to be achieved

## Positioning in the Wiki

Tracy is the most explicitly tactical author in the wiki. Where Covey provides the philosophical architecture (Q2, character, maturity) and Kotler provides the neuroscience (flow, motivation stack), Tracy provides the daily habit routines: what to do at 8am, how to structure a task list, how to beat tomorrow's avoidance today.

The three levels are complementary: **why** (values, character) → **what** (priority frameworks) → **how** (daily practices).
