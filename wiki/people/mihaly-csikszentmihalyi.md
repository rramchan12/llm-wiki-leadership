# Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

**Role:** Hungarian-American psychologist (1934–2021). One of the founders of positive psychology. Coined the concept of "flow" and spent decades researching optimal experience through the Experience Sampling Method (ESM). Distinguished Professor of Psychology at Claremont Graduate University.

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Csikszentmihalyi is the intellectual origin point for all flow-related content in this wiki. His challenge-skills model, autotelic personality concept, and ESM research created the foundation that Kotler later extended with neuroscience and trigger identification. Without Csikszentmihalyi's work, the entire peak-performance branch of this wiki would not exist.

## Key Contributions

- **[[flow-states|Flow]]** — Defined and named the state of optimal experience. Challenge-skills balance as the core mechanism.
- **Experience Sampling Method (ESM)** — Pioneered the methodology for studying subjective experience in real time. Key finding: people are happiest during flow but spend most time in apathy.
- **Autotelic personality** — The trainable orientation toward experience that transforms routine into engagement. Maps to [[intrinsic-motivation]].
- **Paradox of work** — People experience more flow at work than at leisure, yet report wanting to be elsewhere. Active challenge produces better states than passive relaxation.
- **Complexity and growth** — Flow produces a more differentiated and integrated self. A life rich in flow is one of continuous psychological growth.
