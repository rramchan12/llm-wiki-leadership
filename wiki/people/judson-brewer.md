# Judson Brewer

**Role:** Neuroscientist, psychiatrist, Director of Research and Innovation at Brown University's Mindfulness Center. Pioneer in using mindfulness and habit-loop science to treat anxiety and addiction. TEDx speaker ("A Simple Way to Break a Bad Habit" — 19M+ views).

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Brewer bridges neuroscience and practical self-help. He explains *why* anxiety persists (the brain's reward-based learning system) and *how* to change it (update the reward value, replace with curiosity) in a way that doesn't require willpower or positive thinking. His work complements both Burns (cognitive content level) and Harris (relationship-to-thoughts level) by adding the neurological mechanism level.

## Key Contributions

- **[[habit-loops]]** — The trigger → behavior → reward model applied to anxiety, addiction, and all persistent behaviors.
- **Reward-Based Learning** — The brain repeats behaviors it associates with reward value. Update the value, change the behavior.
- **Bigger Better Offer (BBO)** — Curiosity as the replacement behavior that genuinely outcompetes worry.
- **Disenchantment** — Clearly seeing that a behavior doesn't deliver what it promises. The brain naturally drops disenchanted behaviors.
