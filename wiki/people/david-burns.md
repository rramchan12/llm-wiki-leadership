# David D. Burns

**Role:** Psychiatrist, Adjunct Clinical Professor Emeritus at Stanford University School of Medicine. Pioneer in popularizing Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for the general public. Author of *Feeling Good: The New Mood Therapy* — one of the most prescribed books by mental health professionals.

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Burns made CBT accessible to millions. His 10 cognitive distortions framework is the foundational diagnostic lens for understanding how thinking errors create negative moods, bad decisions, and stuck relationships. Every other book in the mental health cluster (Harris, Brewer) either builds on, complements, or offers an alternative to Burns's approach.

## Key Contributions

- **10 Cognitive Distortions** — The taxonomy of twisted thinking patterns. See [[cognitive-distortions]].
- **Triple-Column Technique** — The core daily practice: automatic thought → distortion → rational response.
- **Daily Mood Log** — Extended tracking tool for thought-mood-behavior connections.
- **Empirical validation** — Helped establish the evidence base showing CBT equals or exceeds medication for depression.
