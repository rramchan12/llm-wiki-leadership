# Cal Newport

**Role:** Computer Science professor at Georgetown University; author of *Deep Work* (2016), *So Good They Can't Ignore You* (2012), *Digital Minimalism* (2019), and *A World Without Email* (2021). Research focuses on the theory of focused work, career capital, and digital minimalism.

---

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Newport is the most systematic thinker on the intersection of focused attention, skill development, and knowledge-worker productivity. His work provides the operational framework for what Kotler calls "ruthless prioritization" and what Csikszentmihalyi's flow research shows about the necessity of challenge-at-the-edge: Newport tells you exactly what to do with your schedule to make both possible.

His key contribution to this wiki is the bridge between *what* high performers protect (their focused attention) and *how* to structurally protect it in modern organizational life.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Deep Work / Shallow Work distinction** — Qualitative distinction between value-creating and maintenance work; see [[deep-work-vs-shallow-work]]
- **Attention Residue** — The cognitive cost of context-switching; why fragmented work days produce low-quality output
- **Four Philosophies** — Monastic, Bimodal, Rhythmic, Journalistic approaches to scheduling deep work
- **Shutdown Ritual** — The end-of-day protocol that enables genuine recovery and productive unconscious processing
