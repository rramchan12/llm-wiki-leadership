# Bill Perkins

**Role:** Entrepreneur, hedge fund manager, poker player, and author of *Die With Zero: Getting All You Can from Your Money and Your Life* (2020).

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Perkins offers a secular, pragmatic, experience-centered framework for life planning that directly challenges the deferral culture implicit in most financial and career planning advice. His core provocation — "maximize experiences, not net worth" — is not anti-wealth; it is a reorientation of the *purpose* of wealth toward the experiences that compose a well-lived life.

For this wiki, his contribution sits in productive tension with the Stoic cluster (which counsels detachment from experience) and supplements the Peak Performance cluster (which optimizes for flow and performance but doesn't directly address cross-life-stage planning).

## Key Contributions

- **Memory dividend:** Experiences pay memory returns that compound for the rest of life — earlier is more. [[memory-dividend]]
- **Time buckets:** Life stage-specific experience planning; certain experiences are time-windowed. [[time-buckets]]
- **Health/wealth mismatch:** Wealth arrives later than the capacity to enjoy it; deliberate correction required. [[perkins-die-with-zero]]
- **Give while living:** Shift inheritance and giving left in time so recipients can use it and you can witness the impact. [[perkins-die-with-zero]]
- **Die with zero:** The goal is to exhaust life potential before money; not to die rich with unlived life behind you. [[perkins-die-with-zero]]

## Biography Notes

- Grew up in humble circumstances; entered the energy industry and became a successful hedge fund manager and commodities trader.
- *Die With Zero* was inspired by watching wealthy, capable people defer life experiences to retirement and then find their health or circumstances limited when the time came.
- Known in poker circles; the calculated-risk mindset from professional poker informs the book's framework.
