# Robert Sapolsky

**Role:** Professor of Biology and Neuroscience at Stanford University; MacArthur Fellow; primatologist; author of *Behave* (2017), *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers*, and *A Primate's Memoir*. One of the world's leading researchers on stress, the brain, and the biology of human behavior.

---

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Sapolsky provides the deepest scientific substrate available for understanding why people behave the way they do — a foundation that other leadership books treat impressionistically or intuitively. *Behave* explains the biology underneath nearly every leadership challenge: why smart people make bad decisions under stress, why teams develop "us vs. them" cultures, why the same person behaves heroically in one context and selfishly in another.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Limbic Override** — The amygdala fires before the prefrontal cortex can evaluate a situation; stress, fear, and chronic cortisol degrade the brain's judgment organ. See [[limbic-override]].
- **In-Group / Out-Group Dynamics** — Automatic sorting into "us" and "them" with profoundly different moral processing, driven partly by oxytocin's parochial amplification. See [[in-group-out-group-dynamics]].
- **Context over Character** — The most fully evidenced challenge to the Fundamental Attribution Error in leadership literature: context explains behavioral variance more than dispositional traits in most real settings.
- **Gene-Environment Interaction** — Genes set ranges, not destinies; early environment co-produces the dispositional person.
