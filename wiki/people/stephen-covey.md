# Stephen R. Covey

**Role:** Author, educator, leadership consultant; globally influential for the Seven Habits framework.

## Who He Is

Stephen Richards Covey (1932–2012) was an American educator, author, businessman, and keynote speaker. He holds an MBA from Harvard and a doctorate from Brigham Young University, where he also taught. He co-founded FranklinCovey, a global leadership development company.

*The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People* (1989) is one of the best-selling nonfiction books in history, with over 40 million copies sold. It remains a foundational text in leadership development, personal effectiveness, and organizational culture.

## Key Works

- **The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People** (1989) — foundational; the maturity continuum, seven habits, character ethic
- **First Things First** (1994) — deep expansion of Habit 3; time management matrix; Quadrant II living
- **Principle-Centered Leadership** (1990) — applying the seven habits at organizational scale
- **The 8th Habit** (2004) — extending the model: find your voice and inspire others to find theirs

## Core Ideas

- **Character ethic vs. personality ethic:** Effectiveness is built on deep principles, not technique
- **Maturity continuum:** Dependence → Independence → Interdependence (see [[maturity-continuum]])
- **Time management matrix:** Q2 (important, not urgent) is where effectiveness lives (see [[time-management-matrix]])
- **Emotional bank account:** Trust is a resource that must be invested in before it can be drawn on (see [[emotional-bank-account]])
- **Circle of Influence:** Focus on what you can affect (see [[circle-of-influence]])

## Intellectual Context

Covey was deeply influenced by Viktor Frankl (freedom of choice between stimulus and response), management literature of the 1980s, and his deep roots in principle-based thinking. The Seven Habits synthesized a semester's worth of leadership learning into a framework accessible to individuals and organizations alike.

His work bridges self-help (personal habits, character development) and organizational leadership — an unusual combination that gives the book unusual durability.
