# Naval Ravikant

**Role:** Co-founder and former CEO of AngelList; prolific angel investor (Uber, Twitter, Notion, and hundreds of others); philosopher-entrepreneur whose collected wisdom was compiled by Eric Jorgenson in *The Almanack of Naval Ravikant* (2020).

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Naval occupies a distinctive position: a highly successful technologist and investor who thinks deeply about both wealth creation *and* inner philosophy — and argues they are compatible. His frameworks synthesize classical philosophy (especially Stoicism), modern entrepreneurship theory (leverage, compounding), and contemplative practice (meditation, presence) into actionable principles accessible without spiritual commitment.

For this wiki, Naval is the bridge between the Stoic cluster (Seneca, Aurelius, Frankl on inner life) and the Performance cluster (Kotler, Csikszentmihalyi on output and achievement). He argues that genuine happiness and genuine wealth-building are both *internal* projects before they are external ones.

## Key Contributions

- **Specific knowledge:** The untrained, idiosyncratic knowledge at your unique intersection of curiosity and skill — the source of durable advantage. [[specific-knowledge]]
- **Leverage (four types):** Labor, capital, code, and media as force multipliers. Code and media are permission-less. [[leverage]]
- **Happiness as a skill:** Internal practice, not external achievement. Presence over acquisition. [[happiness-as-skill]]
- **Long-term games:** Compound wealth, relationships, and reputation by choosing long-term partners and sticking. [[jorgenson-naval-almanack]]
- **Four types of luck:** Blind luck → hustle luck → skill luck → character luck (the goal). [[jorgenson-naval-almanack]]
- **Productize yourself:** Specific knowledge + accountability + leverage = scalable wealthy output. [[jorgenson-naval-almanack]]

## Biography Notes

- Born in India; immigrated to the United States as a child.
- Founded AngelList (2010), which transformed startup fundraising.
- One of Silicon Valley's most successful angel investors with an exceptional track record.
- Became known for Twitter threads and podcast appearances (especially a ~2018 tweetstorm on "How to Get Rich") that distilled his philosophy with unusual clarity and generosity.
- *The Almanack* (2020) was compiled by Eric Jorgenson as a public service — Naval made it freely available.
