# Ray Dalio

**Role:** Founder of Bridgewater Associates (the world's largest hedge fund as of 2017); retired Co-Chairman. Author of *Principles: Life and Work* (2017) and *Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises* (2018). One of the most prominent proponents of systematic, algorithmic organizational design.

---

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Dalio represents the most fully developed implementation of explicit-principles leadership in the business world. Unlike most leadership authors who describe practices, Dalio built and ran an organization of 1,500+ people for four decades on a fully documented, explicit principles-as-OS framework.

His significance: he demonstrates that the costs of radical transparency (discomfort, psychological challenge) are outweighed by the benefits (faster organizational learning, better decision quality, accountability that is structural rather than dependent on the leader's mood) at large scale.

---

## Key Contributions

- **5-Step Process** — Goals → Problems → Diagnosis → Design → Execute; the repeating cycle of improvement
- **Radical Transparency** — Make reasoning and data visible; eliminate information asymmetry games. See [[radical-transparency-and-algorithms]].
- **Believability-Weighted Decision-Making** — Input weighted by track record, not seniority
- **The Machine Model** — Leader designs and diagnoses the organization as a machine producing outcomes
- **"Pain + Reflection = Progress"** — Suffering is only valuable when systematically converted to principle
- **Principle-Building** — After every significant failure or success, write the principle that generalizes it
