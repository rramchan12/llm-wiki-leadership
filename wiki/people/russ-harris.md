# Russ Harris

**Role:** Medical practitioner turned psychotherapist. One of the world's leading trainers in Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT). Author of *The Happiness Trap*, *The Confidence Gap*, *ACT Made Simple*, and other ACT resources. Based in Australia.

## Why He Matters to This Wiki

Harris is the primary translator of ACT for a general audience. While Steven Hayes developed the academic framework, Harris made it practical and accessible. His work provides the key alternative to Burns's CBT: instead of changing thought *content*, change your *relationship* to thoughts. This distinction matters deeply for leadership, self-management, and navigating difficult emotions.

## Key Contributions

- **[[psychological-flexibility]]** — The six-process model: defusion, acceptance, present moment, self-as-context, values, committed action.
- **[[cognitive-defusion]]** — Techniques for unhooking from thoughts without challenging their content.
- **[[values-based-action]]** — Distinguishing values (directions) from goals (destinations); acting from what matters.
- **The CHOICE Point** — A simple decision model: toward values or away from pain?
